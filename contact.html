FAQ

Frequenty asked questions

How long does an air conditioner typically last?

With proper maintenance, an air conditioner can last 10 to 15 years. Regular cleaning and servicing can help extend its lifespan and maintain efficiency.

What’s the difference between an inverter and a non-inverter air conditioner?

An inverter AC adjusts its compressor speed to maintain the desired temperature, making it more energy-efficient and quieter. A non-inverter AC operates at a fixed speed, consuming more power as it repeatedly turns on and off to regulate temperature.

Can I install an air conditioner by myself?

While some window-type ACs are easy to install, it's highly recommended to have a professional install your unit to ensure proper placement, secure mounting, and correct electrical connections.

Why is my air conditioner leaking water?

Water leakage can be due to a clogged drain line, dirty filters, or low refrigerant levels. If your AC is leaking, turn it off and contact a technician to prevent further damage.
